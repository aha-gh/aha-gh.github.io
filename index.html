Many first-rate benefits can be derived from the use of elevation data, especially over large areas of land.
Route planning tools will never give optimum results in complex regions if they ignore the slopes to which vehicles are subjected.
The flood risk prediction has continued for 40 years using the same methods without taking into account the precise topography of the catchment basins and areas of obstructions identified.
And this is only the most visible part of what the world can extract from the data obtained from new technologies to provide the planning tools for an effectively sustainable development.
We are here to promote the use of this complex and intelligent data.
After two centuries of producing data for military purposes, flat and inert, we are convinced that this is the right way to work in a finished world with 70 million displaced people, to promote peace and reconstruction.
